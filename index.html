Sleepiness contributes to approximately 1 in 5 fatal road accidents.
If a driver falls asleep for just four seconds while travelling at 100 km/h, the car will have travelled 111 metres without a driver in control.
Around 30% of severe single vehicle crashes in rural areas involve the driver being fatigued.
In Victoria each year, fatigue results in about 50 deaths and approximately 300 serious injuries.
Drivers who have been awake for 24 hours will have a driving performance similar to a person who has a Blood Alcohol Concentration of 0.1 g/100ml. Those drivers are seven times more likely to have an accident.

Signs of Fatigue

Early warning signs include:

  • Having wandering thoughts
  • Missing a gear
  • Slowing down unintentionally
  • Braking too late

Danger signs include:

  • Yawning
  • Blinking more than usual
  • Having trouble keeping your head up
  • Eyes closing for a moment
  • Eyes going out of focus
  • Forgetting about driving in the previous few minutes

If you experience a danger sign, then you should pull off the road immediately; take a short rest; and/or let another person drive.

Causes of Fatigue include:

  • Not having had enough sleep
  • Driving when you would normally be asleep
  • Sleeping disorders

How to avoid Fatigue

  • Get a good night's sleep - don't stay up all night
  • Take regular breaks
  • Avoid travelling more than 8 hours in a day
  • Share the drive
  • Don't drive when you would usually be sleeping

You can also pull your vehicle to a safe parking place and take a short nap, if you start to feel tired.
